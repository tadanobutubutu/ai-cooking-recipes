# Shakshuka

**⚠ Allergens**: eggs
**🕐Cooking Time**: 25 minutes
**👥Servings**: 2

## Ingredients
- Olive oil: 2 tbsp
- Yellow onion, diced: 120 g
- Red bell pepper, diced: 120 g
- Garlic, minced: 2 cloves (about 6 g)
- Tomato paste: 1 tbsp
- Ground cumin: 1 tsp
- Sweet paprika: 1 tsp
- Chili flakes: 0.25 tsp
- Canned crushed tomatoes: 400 g
- Salt: 0.5 tsp
- Black pepper: 0.25 tsp
- Large eggs: 4 (about 200 g)
- Fresh parsley, chopped: 1 tbsp

## Instructions
1. Heat the olive oil in a 25 cm (10-inch) skillet over medium heat.
2. Add the diced onion and bell pepper and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
3. Stir in the garlic, tomato paste, cumin, paprika, and chili flakes, and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
4. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, season with salt and black pepper, and bring the sauce to a gentle simmer.
5. Simmer uncovered for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened slightly.
6. Use the back of a spoon to make 4 shallow wells in the sauce, then crack one egg into each well.
7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and cook for 5 to 7 minutes until the egg whites are fully set and the yolks reach your preferred doneness.
8. Remove the skillet from the heat, sprinkle with chopped parsley, and serve immediately with crusty bread.

## Safety Notes
- Use fresh, refrigerated eggs and cook until the whites are completely set (no translucent areas) to reduce the risk of Salmonella from undercooked egg.
- If you prefer fully cooked yolks for food safety, continue cooking covered until the yolks reach an internal temperature of 160°F (71°C).
- Refrigerate any leftovers in an airtight container within 2 hours of cooking and consume within 2 days; reheat thoroughly before eating.
