# Classic Pancakes

**⚠️ Allergens**: eggs / dairy / gluten
**🕐 Cooking Time**: 20 minutes
**👥 Servings**: 2

## Ingredients
- All-purpose flour: 150 g
- Granulated sugar: 2 tbsp
- Baking powder: 2 tsp
- Salt: 0.5 tsp
- Whole milk: 240 ml
- Large egg: 1 (about 50 g)
- Unsalted butter, melted: 30 g
- Vanilla extract: 1 tsp
- Neutral oil (for the pan): 1 tbsp

## Instructions
1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt until evenly combined.
2. In a separate bowl, whisk the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract until smooth.
3. Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and whisk gently just until no dry flour remains; a few small lumps are fine.
4. Let the batter rest for 5 minutes so the baking powder activates and the gluten relaxes.
5. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat and lightly coat it with the neutral oil, wiping away any excess with a paper towel.
6. Pour about 60 ml (1/4 cup) of batter per pancake onto the skillet, leaving space between each one.
7. Cook for about 2 to 3 minutes, until bubbles form across the surface and the edges look set.
8. Flip each pancake once and cook for another 1 to 2 minutes, until golden brown and cooked through in the center.
9. Transfer the pancakes to a plate and serve immediately with butter and maple syrup, or your preferred toppings.

## Safety Notes
- Use fresh, refrigerated eggs and cook the pancakes until the centers are fully set (no wet batter) to reduce the risk of Salmonella from undercooked egg.
- Keep the heat at medium-low; pancakes cooked on high heat can brown on the outside while remaining raw in the middle.
- Store any leftover pancakes in an airtight container in the refrigerator within 2 hours of cooking and consume within 3 days.
