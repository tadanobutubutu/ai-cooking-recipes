# Egg Fried Rice

**⚠️ Allergens**: eggs / soy / sesame
**🕐 Cooking Time**: 15 minutes
**👥 Servings**: 2

## Ingredients
- Cooked, cold day-old white rice: 400 g
- Large eggs, beaten: 3 (about 150 g)
- Scallions, thinly sliced (white and green parts separated): 4 (about 40 g)
- Garlic cloves, minced: 2 (about 6 g)
- Frozen peas and carrots, thawed: 100 g
- Neutral cooking oil (such as vegetable or canola): 2 tbsp
- Toasted sesame oil: 1 tsp
- Soy sauce: 1.5 tbsp
- Salt: 0.5 tsp
- White pepper: 0.25 tsp

## Instructions
1. Break up any clumps in the cold cooked rice with your hands or a fork so every grain is separated.
2. Heat 1 tbsp of the neutral oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat until shimmering.
3. Pour in the beaten eggs and scramble them quickly with a spatula until just set but still glossy, about 30 seconds, then transfer to a plate.
4. Add the remaining 1 tbsp of neutral oil to the pan, then stir in the white parts of the scallions and the minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add the peas and carrots and stir-fry for 1 minute to heat through.
6. Add the rice and press it into an even layer, letting it toast undisturbed for about 1 minute before stirring, then repeat once more so the grains take on a light color.
7. Drizzle the soy sauce and sesame oil around the edge of the pan and toss everything together to coat the rice evenly.
8. Return the scrambled eggs to the pan, breaking them into bite-sized pieces, and season with the salt and white pepper.
9. Stir in the green parts of the scallions, toss for 15 seconds, and serve immediately.

## Safety Notes
- Use rice that has been refrigerated (not left at room temperature) within 1 hour of cooking to reduce the risk of Bacillus cereus; reheat to at least 165°F (75°C).
- Cook the eggs until they are no longer runny so the whites and yolks are fully set.
- Do not reheat this fried rice more than once, and consume leftovers within 1 day.
